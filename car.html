🇮🇳 Indian Classic & Iconic Cars

Hindustan Landmaster

1954 - 1956

Launched in 1954, the Hindustan Landmaster was an Indian-built version of the Morris Oxford Series II. It featured a 1.5L petrol engine and was known for its durability, rounded design, and chrome-heavy styling. As a predecessor to the iconic Ambassador, it marked India’s early journey into domestically assembled passenger cars.

Hindustan Motors

Hindustan Ambassador

1958 - 2014

A Licensed Version of Morris Oxford III The King of Indian Roads, the Ambassador was India's most iconic car, serving as the official government vehicle for decades.

Hindustan Motors

Premier Padmini / Premier President

1964 - 2000

A Licensed Version of the Fiat 1100 Delight. Mumbai's beloved taxi, the Padmini became synonymous with Indian city transport and Indian films.

Premier Automobiles

Maruti 800

1983 - 2014

The People's Car. India's first modern small car that put the nation on wheels and democratized car ownership in India.

Maruti Suzuki

Tata Sierra

1991 - 2000

India's first SUV. A bold design statement that introduced Indians to sport utility vehicles with its distinctive three-door configuration.

Tata Motors

Hindustan Contessa

1984 - 2002

The Muscle Car of India. Based on Vauxhall Victor, it was India's answer to luxury sedans with its imposing presence.

Hindustan Motors

Mahindra Jeep

1947 - Present

The Original Indian 4x4 based on Willy's CJ 3A. Built under license from Willys, it became the backbone of rural India and off-road adventures.

Mahindra & Mahindra

Standard Herald

1961 - 1974

The Standard Herald, introduced in India in 1961 by Standard Motors, was based on the UK’s Triumph Herald. It featured a rear-wheel-drive layout, a turning radius-friendly chassis, and later a 4-door version unique to India. Known for its distinctive bonnet and classic British styling, it became a niche icon in Indian motoring history.

Standard Automobiles

TATA Sumo

1994 - 2019

Launched in 1994, the Tata Sumo was a rugged, rear-wheel-drive SUV designed for Indian roads and utility use. With a boxy design and spacious seating for 7–10 passengers, it gained popularity among families, government fleets, and rural transport. Known for durability and affordability, it became a Tata Motors workhorse icon.

TATA Motors

🌍 Global Classic & Iconic Cars

Ferrari 250 GTO

1962 - 1964

The Holy Grail of classic cars. Only 36 were made, combining racing pedigree with stunning beauty. Now worth tens of millions.

Origin: Italy

Porsche 911

1963 - Present

The timeless sports car. Its distinctive silhouette has remained virtually unchanged, defining the perfect sports car formula.

Origin: Germany

Volkswagen Type 1 / Volkswagen Beetle

1938 - 2003

The People's Car. Originally Hitler's vision, it became a symbol of peace, love, and counterculture in the 1960s.

Origin: Germany

Jaguar E-Type

1961 - 1975

The most beautiful car ever made. Enzo Ferrari called it "the most beautiful car ever made" - combining performance with elegance.

Origin: United Kingdom

Mini Cooper

1959 - 2000

Small car, big personality. Revolutionized small car design and became a cultural icon, from Monte Carlo rallies to movies.

Origin: United Kingdom

Morris Minor

1948 - 1971

The Morris Minor is a classic British compact car, produced from 1948 to 1971. Known for its charming curves, reliability, and innovative design, it became an icon of affordable motoring and British post-war engineering, beloved by enthusiasts worldwide.

Origin: United Kingdom

Ford Model T

1908 - 1927

The Ford Model T, launched in 1908, revolutionized transportation with its affordability and its the first mass produced car. Known as the first car for the common man, it featured a simple, rugged design and changed the automotive industry forever.

Origin: United States

Mercedes Benz 126 - S Class

1972 – 1980

The Mercedes-Benz W116, produced from 1972 to 1980, was the first official S-Class. It featured advanced safety innovations, luxurious interiors, and powerful engines, setting a new benchmark in executive sedans with unmatched German engineering, comfort, and prestige.

Origin: Germany

Mercedes Benz W123 - Executive Class

1976 – 1986

The Mercedes-Benz W123 is a legendary midsize luxury sedan known for its timeless design, unmatched durability, and smooth ride. Produced from 1976 to 1986, it's beloved for its reliability, making it a global icon of automotive engineering excellence.

Origin: Germany

Mercedes Benz S600 Pullman Guard

2007 – 2013

A luxury armored limousine built for heads of state, the S600 Pullman Guard offers maximum protection with VR9-level armor. Powered by a V12 engine, it combines Mercedes' signature comfort and technology with reinforced security, including bulletproof glass and bomb resistance, making it a fortress on wheels.

Origin: Germany

Rolls Royce Silver Cloud

1955 – 1958

Introduced in 1955, the Silver Cloud I represents Rolls-Royce elegance with its smooth inline-six engine, graceful lines, and handcrafted interiors. Built for royalty and elites, it featured a body-on-frame design allowing custom coachbuilding. The car balanced performance with old-world luxury, setting the tone for post-war British automotive prestige.

Origin: United Kingdom

Rolls Royce Phantom IV

1955 – 1958

The rarest Rolls-Royce, the Phantom IV was produced exclusively for royalty and heads of state between 1950–1956. Only 18 units were made. It featured a massive straight-eight engine and custom bodywork, offering unparalleled luxury, prestige, and exclusivity. Its presence symbolized power, discretion, and supreme craftsmanship.

Origin: United Kingdom
